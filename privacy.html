2.3 Services and features

PottyMap is a location-based social game that transforms the daily dog walk into a competitive, collectible, and community-driven experience. The core gameplay turns your neighborhood into a contested map. As you walk your dog and log each pee or poop, that mark claims the 25-meter grid cell it lands in — staking your territory on a shared map. Marks stay active for 72 hours, and re-marking a cell you already hold extends its life on a sliding scale, so regular walkers naturally defend their turf. Each cell ranks its owners by how long their claim will last, and the top three holders earn a point for every hour they keep their position. String enough adjacent cells together and you can claim and name an entire neighborhood, which everyone sees on the map for 12 hours. It's Splatoon-meets-dog-walking: the more consistently you walk, the more of the map you own. A real points economy and collectible system sit on top of the map. You earn points by holding territory and by logging in — a 7-day daily-reward streak escalates from points to rare and epic prizes. You spend those points on a gacha-style Dog Food Card system: several collectible cards across three families (Bone Chew, Power Kibble, Trail Feast) and four rarities, with weighted draw odds and a pity timer that guarantees a rare pull after enough misses. Equip up to three cards before a walk, then attach one to a mark to extend its territory timer or multiply your points. Cards can be drawn, bought outright, or crafted from fragments. Progress is also recognized through live-computed titles like Marathon Paws (100 km walked), Land Baron (50+ active territories), and Social Butterfly — badges that appear on your profile. The social layer connects you to the dog walkers around you. You can add friends and chat in real time with text, photos, and voice notes, or mark rival walkers as foes so their territory shows up on your map as turf to watch. A community feed presents walk photos in a browsable grid you can filter by distance or friends-only, with likes, threaded comments, and a "show on map" button that pins exactly where each photo was taken. When you start a walk, a socialization report surfaces nearby friends, foe zones — and a live heatmap overlay shows where the neighborhood's dogs are most active. Thoughtful polish rounds it out: an iOS lock-screen widget lets you log a pee, poop, or photo without unlocking your phone; high-precision GPS keeps your route clean even when stationary; a shareable Walk Recap card your dog's photo; and content moderation (a proactive text filter plus a reporting system) keeps the community safe. In short, PottyMap makes every walk count — turning a daily chore into a territory game, a collection grind, a fitness tracker, and a neighborhood social network all at once. Some features described in the Terms or policies may not be available during beta, phased launch, or in all locations. Advertising, targeted advertising, subscriptions, paid purchases, order fulfillment, reward redemption, payment processors, and certain commercial features apply only if they are actually enabled in the Services.
